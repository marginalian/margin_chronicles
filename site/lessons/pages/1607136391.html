The Hainish Clinic

Learn proper usage and compelling theories from one of science fiction's most acclaimed Grand Masters, Ursula K. Le Guin.


Learn, Compare, Practice

  • Read Steering the Craft alongside Five Ways to Forgiveness using the following pattern: after 2 chapters of SC, read one of the stories in FWF. Look for examples of what was introduced in SC within the stories. Keep notes.
  • Complete the assignments within SC.
  • (Optional) Read Conversations on Writing. This collection of three interviews—particularly the first one—covers the subject matter in Steering the Craft. It explores Le Guin's thoughts about those topics in a bit more detail, but offers few new ideas.

Refine

Read the following essays that Le Guin wrote:

  • The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction
  • Talking About Writing
  • Myth and Archetype in Science Fiction

Between essays, read a piece of Hainish-cycle short fiction found within these collections:

  • The Birthday of the World
  • The Wind's Twelve Quarters

Extra

  • Read the essay/story combo by Le Guin in Those Who Can by Robin Scott Wilson.
  • Write a flash fiction story emulating something from the story.
